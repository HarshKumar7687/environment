What we do with the waste?

  • Composting:

    Convert biodegradable waste into nutrient-rich compost for gardening and farming.

  • Biogas Production:

    Use organic waste to produce biogas, a renewable energy source.

  • Vermiculture

    Employ worms to break down waste into high-quality organic fertilizer.

  • Mulching

    Spread organic waste like leaves and grass clippings on soil to retain moisture and enrich the soil.

  • Animal Feed

    Use certain types of biodegradable waste as feed for animals, like vegetable scraps for livestock.

  • Bio-Fertilizers

    Turn organic waste into bio-fertilizers to improve soil health.

  • Biofuel Production

    Process biodegradable waste into biofuels that can power vehicles and machinery.

  • Waste-to-Energy Conversion

    Utilize waste in advanced processes to generate electricity or heat energy.

  • Craft Material

    Use biodegradable waste for DIY crafts, such as creating paper or eco-friendly decorations.

  • Eco-Friendly Packaging

    Process biodegradable waste into packaging materials like paper or biodegradable plastic alternatives.

  • Recycling: Many non-biodegradable materials like plastic, metal, and glass can be recycled and used to make new products.
  • Eco-Bricks: Used plastic bottles can be filled with smaller non-biodegradable waste to create eco-bricks for construction.
  • Upcycling: Transform old, non-biodegradable items such as tires or plastic containers into usable items like furniture or decor.
  • Art Projects: Waste materials can be creatively used in art, sculptures, or DIY home decor projects.
  • Road Construction: Some non-biodegradable waste, like plastic waste, can be used in making durable road surfaces.
  • Energy Production: Certain non-biodegradable wastes can be incinerated in waste-to-energy plants to generate electricity.
  • Reuse: Items like plastic bags and containers can be cleaned and reused multiple times before disposal.
  • Building Materials: Waste glass, plastic, and rubber can be incorporated into the production of tiles, insulation, or other building materials.
  • Furniture Creation: Old car tires, plastic barrels, and other items can be repurposed into functional furniture like chairs, tables, or swings.
  • Recycling: Break down devices to extract valuable metals like gold, copper, and silver.
  • Donating: Donate old but functioning devices to charities, schools, or those in need.
  • Upcycling: Use parts of old electronics for creative projects, like making art or home decor.
  • Refurbishing: Repair and resell old devices to give them a second life.
  • Component Harvesting: Salvage working components (batteries, screens, etc.) to repair other devices.
  • Eco-friendly Disposal: Send e-waste to certified recycling centers that ensure proper disposal.
  • Trade-in Programs: advantage of trade-in offers from manufacturers for discounts on new products.
  • Data Erasure & Resale: Erase data securely from old devices and sell them in the second-hand market.
  • DIY Projects: Build small gadgets like robots or simple IoT devices using old circuit boards and parts.
  • Electronic waste recycling: Hazardous components like lead and mercury can be recycled for use in new products.
  • Battery recycling: Recycling facilities extract valuable metals such as lithium and nickel from batteries.
  • Used oil re-refining: Used motor oil can be cleaned and reused as lubricants or other products.
  • Industrial solvents reuse: Some hazardous solvents can be reclaimed and reused in manufacturing processes.
  • Energy recovery: Certain hazardous wastes can be burned as fuel in industrial boilers, cement kilns, etc.
  • Medical Applications: Used in radiation therapy for cancer treatment.
  • Industrial Applications: Employed in non-destructive testing and gauging devices.
  • Power Generation: Certain types can be used in radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs).
  • Research Purposes: Utilized in various scientific studies and experiments.
  • Tracer Studies: Helps in tracing the flow of materials in processes.
  • Construction Materials: Some materials can be incorporated into concrete for specific structural benefits.
